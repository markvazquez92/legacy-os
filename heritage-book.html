How We Research — Complete Transparency

Where the Information Comes From (And What We Can't Verify)

We use five core archives: FamilySearch (the world's largest genealogy database — 100% free, run by the LDS Church), U.S. Census records (1850–1950 available online), border crossing documents (NARA archives, El Paso & Nogales crossings), Church parish records (baptisms, marriages, deaths — especially strong for Mexico and Spain), and Spanish nobility registries (for hidalgo status and historical context).

What we can verify: Birth/death dates and locations (when documented), migration patterns, occupations, family structure, and cultural/historical context of their regions and era.

What we can't: Pre-1850 genealogy gets difficult fast — records don't exist or are archived in Spanish/Mexican government offices we can't access online. Oral family history (stories about why they left, who they knew) — we include what you tell us, but we verify what we can in the archives.

You stay in control: Before we write your book, we show you everything we found. You verify it. You correct it. If something doesn't match your family's story, we change it. This is your history.
