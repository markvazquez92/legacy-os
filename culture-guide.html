Culture Guide

Your Cultural DNA
4-Week Deep Dive

Stories, not lectures. Authentic. Personal. Celebratory. Understand where you come from. Understand why you're the way you are. Pass it down.

"This is your family's cultural DNA. Recipes you remember but can't quite place. Words your grandmother said that no one translates anymore. A photo of a village you've never seen. We build the map."

The Four Weeks

Week 01

Where We Came From

Maps, ancestry, and the reason they left. Tracing origins back as far as records allow — villages, ports, paperwork, the towns that shaped your last name. Why did they leave? What did they carry with them? What did they leave behind that's still alive in you?

  • Origin maps — your ancestral regions plotted
  • Ancestry records — ship manifests, census, church records
  • Why they left — war, famine, opportunity, love
  • Immigration stories — the journey itself
Week 02

How We Adapted

Recipes evolved. Language shifted. Traditions changed. How did the family build a new identity without erasing the old one? What got preserved, what got rewritten, and what got lost in translation?

  • Recipes then & now — original vs. Americanized
  • Language shifts — words kept, words dropped
  • Holidays remixed — Christmas, Easter, Day of the Dead
  • Hybrid identity — the bicultural middle ground
Week 03

What We Built

Family businesses. First jobs. First homes. Community contributions. The moments of cultural pride that kept the thread alive across generations. The sports team your dad coached. The bakery your tia ran. The church your family helped pay for.

  • Family businesses — trades, shops, ventures
  • First jobs — what they did to land here
  • Community ties — church, union, block, team
  • Pride moments — graduations, weddings, buying the house
Week 04

Passing It Down

Conversations to have with kids and grandkids about roots, identity, and why these stories matter. Scripts for the awkward dinner table. Prompts for the quiet car ride. Ways to make heritage a living thing rather than a museum piece.

  • Kid-friendly scripts — age-specific talking points
  • Family quiz kit — games, trivia, memory tests
  • Photo timelines — visual storytelling
  • Why this matters — the "so what" your grandchildren will ask

What's Included
