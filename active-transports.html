Transport Network

Moving Rescues Together

My Legacy Continues runs a volunteer transport network for rescue dogs across California. Any rescue can post dogs needing transport. Volunteers drive individual legs — you do not need to drive the whole route. This network exists because rescue, return, and euthanasia is a coordination problem. We're building the infrastructure to fix it.

How the Relay Works

01

Rescue Posts a Need

A dog needs to get from City A to City B. The rescue submits a transport request.

02

We Map the Route

We break the trip into legs — usually 1-2 hours each. Each leg gets posted here.

03

Volunteers Claim Legs

You pick a leg near you. Show up, pick up the dog, drive your stretch, hand off to the next volunteer.

04

Dog Gets Home

Every handoff is confirmed. The dog's profile updates with their journey. They make it to their new life.
