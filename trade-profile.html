Our Story

Concrete Pumping the Way It Should Be

Bears Concrete Pumping is a family-run operation out of Santa Maria, California. We pump concrete for residential foundations, commercial slabs, custom pools, hillside builds, and everything in between across Santa Barbara and San Luis Obispo County. Been doing it long enough to know that the job isn't done right until the concrete is where it needs to be.

Mark Vazquez runs every job. When you call Bears, you are talking to the guy who shows up. No middleman, no crews you've never met. If something goes sideways on a pour, the same person who took your call is the one standing next to the pump.

Trailer pumps and line pumps for tight residential lots, backyard pours, elevated decks, and multi-story commercial work. If the concrete has to get somewhere a boom truck can't reach, that's exactly where we do our best work.

Services

What We Do

Residential Foundations

Slabs, footings, and piers for new construction, additions, and ADUs throughout Santa Barbara and SLO County.

Commercial Slabs

Large-volume commercial pours, warehouse floors, parking structures, and tilt-up panel work.

Pools & Custom Pours

Gunite and shotcrete placement for custom pools. Tight-radius line pump work for unconventional site access.

Infrastructure & Public Works

Pump support for municipal projects, drainage structures, retaining walls, and infrastructure rehabilitation.

Emergency Pour Services

Short-notice and after-hours availability. When your schedule tightens or weather changes the plan, call Bears.

Tight-Access & Hillside

Line pump work for lots where conventional boom trucks cannot reach. Hillside, backyard, alley access — no problem.

The DNA of Excellence

Foundations Have Always Been in This Family.

Bears Concrete Pumping didn't come out of nowhere. Mark Vazquez has been working with his hands his whole life — and so did the men before him. The Vazquez family traces back through Nuevo León, Mexico, through colonial Spanish settlements, all the way to confirmed Hidalgo lineage in Galicia and the Basque Country of northern Spain. 1547.

That Hidalgo designation wasn't handed down with money. It was earned through military service, through skilled physical work, through showing up and doing the job right. That's not a coincidence. That's a bloodline.

When Mark pulls up to a job site in Santa Maria, he's carrying five hundred years of people who built things with their hands and didn't cut corners. The Heritage Culture Book tells that whole story. Where it started, how it got here, and why it matters to the next generation.
