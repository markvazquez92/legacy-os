The Mission

From High-Kill Environments to Permanent Safety

California has dozens of high-kill animal shelters — facilities where animals are euthanized not because they are aggressive or sick, but because there is no capacity, no funding, and no one who knows they exist. These animals die anonymous. No name. No story. No record.

The My Legacy Continues Rescue Network changes that. Through a partnership with Underdog Heroes based in Lake Hughes, California, we fund transport logistics that move animals from overcrowded, high-kill environments in the high desert to Santa Barbara County Animal Services (SBCAS), where adoption rates are dramatically higher.

Every animal we register gets a permanent digital profile. A QR-linked ID tag that holds their story, their medical history, their rescue journey, and a link for adoption. We do not let them die unnamed.

The Network Partners

Underdog Heroes

A Lake Hughes, California-based rescue operation that intercepts animals from high-desert kill shelters. They coordinate intake, medical assessment, and transport to higher-adoption-rate facilities throughout California.

underdogheroes.org — Lake Hughes, CA

Santa Barbara County Animal Services

SBCAS operates multiple shelter locations serving Santa Barbara County. Adoption rates here are significantly higher than inland desert facilities due to population density, income levels, and active rescue community engagement.

County-operated partner facility — SB County, CA

The Digital Registry

Every animal that enters this network gets a permanent profile at mylegacycontinues.com. A QR tag links to their page from their collar — viewable by any shelter worker, adopter, or rescuer who scans it. Records do not disappear.

Hosted permanently — zero subscription

Duke arrived at Lake Hughes as a stray surrender with no identification and no medical history on file. He was assessed as a high-priority transport candidate — breed type, size, and shelter capacity made euthanasia likely within 72 hours without intervention.

Underdog Heroes coordinated the transport to SBCAS on March 2026. Duke was vaccinated, neutered, and cleared for adoption. His permanent QR profile was created through the My Legacy Continues registry and is now attached to his collar tag.

Any shelter worker, rescue coordinator, or potential adopter who scans his QR tag gets his full documented history, vaccination records, transport footage, and the direct SBCAS adoption inquiry link. He is no longer anonymous.
