Her Story

The Garcia Line

Debbie Garcia — now Mahony — is the daughter of Elvira "Barbara" Garcia, the youngest of eighteen children born to Francisco and Clara Montemayor Garcia in Sinton, Texas. The Montemayor name traces back to Faustino Montemayor, born 1852, who lived to age 98. That is not coincidence. That is the blood.


Debbie was raised on the Central Coast of California. She built a life here, raised a son here, and her family's story is woven into the fabric of Santa Maria in a way that doesn't get talked about enough. She is the living link between the Montemayor line of South Texas and the Vázquez line of Mexico City — two families that, together, produced Bear.


Her father — Bear's grandfather on the Garcia side — passed when Debbie was only one year old. A loss that left a gap in the oral record, but not in the blood. What she carried forward, she carried alone. And she carried it well.

The Garcia Name

What's in a Name

Garcia is the most common surname in Spain — and it arrived in California with the first wave of Spanish settlers. Mission San Carlos recorded the Garcia name as early as December 13, 1777. Some linguists trace the Garcia name to the Basque word for "bear."


Bear Vázquez. Son of Garcia. Named for the animal his mother's family may have carried in their name for a thousand years. That is not a coincidence anyone planned. That is lineage working through time.
