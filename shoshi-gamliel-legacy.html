Shoshi Gamliel

Founder & President — Underdog Heroes Inc. · Est. 2020 · Riverside, California

Shoshi Gamliel didn’t set out to run a rescue. She set out to save one dog. Then another. Then another. Until her Riverside home became something most people can’t imagine — a house where every room, every corner, every inch of the garage belongs to dogs that the system decided weren’t worth saving.

She founded Underdog Heroes in 2020 with a simple belief: every dog deserves a fair chance. The bully breeds. The mastiffs. The ones with bite histories and medical bills and nobody fighting for them. Shoshi fights for them.

Featured in Newsweek. Covered by Scripps News. Her rescue of Walter — a bulldog found as a stray with chemical burns covering his face — went viral on TikTok and ended with the Osbourne family adopting him. But Shoshi will tell you the ones nobody saw are the ones that matter most.

“I mean, there’s nothing like dogs. I love them more than people, you know?” — Shoshi Gamliel, Scripps News

National Feature

Newsweek

Walter the bulldog was found as a stray with chemical burns covering his face and neck. Shoshi rescued him, covered his surgeries, and nursed him back. The Osbourne family adopted him. The story went viral on TikTok with millions of views.

National Investigation

Scripps News Investigation

Featured in a national investigation on rising shelter euthanasia rates. Shoshi’s fight put a spotlight on Riverside County’s broken system — where euthanasia rates more than doubled from 6% in 2021 to over 15% in 2023.

Why She Does It

Riverside County’s euthanasia rate more than doubled in two years. Shoshi was there when thirty dogs were killed on Valentine’s Day with no warning. She was there when Bowie — a 4-month-old puppy — was mistakenly euthanized before she could reach him. She filed reports. She spoke to press. She kept showing up anyway.

“The harsh reality is we may never break the cycle of cruelty. But we can raise awareness and make an impact.”
